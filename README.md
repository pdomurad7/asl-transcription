## ASL Transcription
A repository for transcription of American Sign Language (ASL) — bridging the gap between the Deaf community and written/spoken English.

#### The Problem
American Sign Language is the primary language of an estimated 1 million people in the United States, with millions more in the broader Deaf and hard-of-hearing community. Yet ASL is a fully independent language — it has its own grammar, syntax, and structure that does not map directly onto written English.
Despite this, most digital tools, voice assistants, and communication platforms are built entirely around spoken or written language. This creates a profound accessibility gap:

ASL users have no reliable way to communicate in real time with hearing people through digital interfaces
Captioning and transcription services focus on speech-to-text, leaving sign language users without equivalent tools
Existing ASL recognition research is fragmented, lacks standardized datasets, and rarely reaches production-ready quality

The result is that millions of people are systematically excluded from everyday digital communication.

#### Why It Matters
Communication is a right, not a privilege
Deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals navigate a world designed around hearing. Automated ASL transcription would reduce dependence on human interpreters — who are expensive, scarce, and unavailable 24/7.
Existing solutions fall short

Human interpreters are not always accessible or affordable
Fingerspelling recognition covers only the alphabet, not full ASL
General gesture recognition models are not trained for the nuances of sign language
Most voice-to-text tools offer nothing for sign language users

The scale of impact
Effective ASL transcription technology could:

Enable real-time communication in healthcare, education, legal, and emergency settings
Make video content, meetings, and customer service accessible
Empower Deaf individuals to interact independently with voice-first interfaces


#### What This Repository Does
This project focuses on building a pipeline for transcribing ASL video input into written English, covering:

Hand shape and movement recognition
Sequence modeling to capture ASL's spatial grammar
Mapping ASL meaning to natural English output
